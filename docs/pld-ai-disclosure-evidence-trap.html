The EU PLD Exposure Test: Defending Against Strict AI Liability

The revised Product Liability Directive brings strict liability rules straight to software, AI models, and automated updates. Will your technical records survive a court-ordered Article 9 disclosure?

Under the new regime, corporate compliance checklists offer zero legal protection. If an automated system causes harm, courts can order your organisation to hand over its internal logs. If your evidence is missing or unreadable, the law automatically presumes your AI was defective. You could lose by default.

The key date is 9 December 2026. From this point forward, the legal burden of proof shifts entirely. If you cannot show the evidence, you are legally liable by default.

The 16-Question Operational Exposure Diagnostic

This diagnostic isolates your immediate exposure to strict liability. It bypasses high-level governance narratives and drills into your actual technical readiness across five blunt operational vectors:

  • Product Status: Are you operating as a passive user, or has your data engineering legally classified you as a primary software producer?
  • Reconstruction: Can your engineering environment instantly reproduce the exact historical model logic from a disputed past case?
  • Traceability: Can you definitively prove the exact telemetry, prompt variants, and raw datasets received by the system at runtime?
  • Disclosure Readiness: Are your internal technical files structured to survive hostile court scrutiny, or will they expose unrelated proprietary secrets?
  • Lifecycle Control: Can you map and verify every automatic patch, model drift variation, and third-party API update over time?

This framework converts abstract legal risks into clear, binary engineering requirements. If your records fail these tests, your legal defense collapses before a trial even begins.
