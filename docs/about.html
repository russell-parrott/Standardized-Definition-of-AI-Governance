    Russell Parrott - Curator of Standardized Definition of AI Governance

    Russell Parrott curates and develops a public body of structural research on AI governance, published under the Creative Commons Attribution–NonCommercial–NoDerivatives 4.0 International licence (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0). His work defines how artificial intelligence, regulation and institutional power interact exposing where governance fails, and designing frameworks that make accountability traceable, enforceable and testable in structure.

    Based in Greece and originally from the United Kingdom, Russell’s doctrine integrates legal design, systems architecture and regulatory economics. Through works such as The Structural Governance Standard for AI and The AI World Order, he develops reference frameworks, analytical models and measurable tests that connect theory to enforcement.

    Russell’s platform operates as an independent centre of analysis for the architecture of AI control; producing open standards, interpretive reports and controlled editions that guide policymakers, regulators and institutions worldwide. He earns through authored works and interpretive editions, not consultancy, ensuring doctrinal independence and structural integrity.

    Readership and Influence

    Russell’s work is followed by AI policy architects, regulators, standards bodies, risk officers and academic researchers across Europe, North & South America and Asia. His analyses are regularly cited in discussions of the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001 and 42119, and AI risk governance frameworks.

    His work is read by regulators, AI policy architects, standards bodies, academic researchers and governance critics across Europe, North & South America and Asia. It circulates among public authorities, financial institutions and intergovernmental bodies; including professionals linked to the European Union, United Nations, International Monetary Fund and national oversight agencies seeking evidence-based doctrine for AI accountability and regulatory enforcement.

    Through this platform AI Governance Theater, Russell publishes structural exposure essays; connecting global AI policy, economic impact and institutional accountability. His writing helps shape emerging doctrine on how governance, verification and enforcement will define the next phase of AI power.
