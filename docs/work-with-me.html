Work with me - Russell Parrott

In most organisations, the language of governance and oversight is already familiar. However, far fewer directors understand that the need to explain and defend decisions years later is usually created, or lost, long before then.

Because a system can appear visible and controlled during normal operations. This creates a corporate belief that the organisation will still be able to explain decisions clearly years later. Very often that turns out to be wrong.

The reason is simple. Most organisational records are created to help run systems, monitor performance and keep operations moving day to day. They are not usually created to explain disputed decisions years later.

My work focuses on one question: when an AI-assisted or automated decision is challenged, what can still be proven later?

What you get

When regulators, courts, insurers, journalists or claimants begin asking what happened, an organisation often discovers the records needed to answer those questions do not exist. By then the system may have changed: vendors change, staff leave and records do get deleted. The people involved may believe they know what happened, but memory and belief are not proof or evidence.

Eventually somebody asks a direct question about a decision and discovers that nobody can clearly answer what actually happened at the time.

Simply put, I explain accountability in plain English.

This is not about searching for hidden failures or arriving with a fixed theory about what an organisation is doing wrong. Every organisation is different. The same is true of how decisions are made, reviewed, recorded and stored..

The problems are not hypothetical and they are not rare. In many organisations they already exist inside normal operations long before anybody realises that proof may be required later.

I do not prescribe governance programmes, operational controls or technical solutions. I explain what accountability actually means before decisions are challenged and proof is demanded later.

When you work with me, you get one of three things:

  1. The Reconstruction Circle – Private monthly papers on what can still be proven after a contested decision. No public publishing. No governance theatre. Just one to two deep analyses delivered privately each month.
  2. One-off explainer – A short, plain-English paper tailored to your organisation's context. No jargon. No recommendations. Just an honest answer to: what can still be proven later?
  3. One-hour conversation – A direct conversation to talk through a specific situation. No document. No follow-up. Just an hour of plain answers about what survives scrutiny.
When scrutiny begins, the question changes very quickly. The issue is no longer whether governance existed. It becomes: can anyone still prove what happened, who decided what and does the surviving evidence still hold together under challenge?
