Most organisations can explain how their AI systems are supposed to work. Far fewer can still explain one exact outcome properly once it is challenged later.

That is the subject of my work.

I write independent books and explanatory material examining what can still be proven after automated and AI-assisted decisions are questioned under legal, regulatory, investigative or public scrutiny. The focus is not governance appearance, policy language or technical aspiration. It is whether one specific outcome can still be reconstructed afterwards from records, authority and retained evidence that already exist.

You are welcome to get in touch regarding publications, citation, licensing, reproduction, reference use or related enquiries connected to the material published here.

Because eventually somebody asks: who decided, why and where is the evidence?
