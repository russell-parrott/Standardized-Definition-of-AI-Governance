    Engagement & Access - Lawful Use of Doctrines

    All doctrines published under the Creative Commons Attribution–NonCommercial–NoDerivatives 4.0 International licence (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0) are free to study, cite and apply within their original form. Their purpose is structural: to expose how systems fail and to provide enforceable methods for verification, refusal, and accountability in AI governance.

    Russell Parrott does not consult, audit, advise or perform private assessments. His work remains independent, interpretive and non-commercial in structure.

    Lawful Forms of Engagement

    1. Controlled Editions
      Licensed print or digital editions prepared for institutional or educational use. Each edition preserves doctrinal integrity while adding lawful reference material, forewords or contextual commentary. Controlled editions never alter doctrine, they clarify provenance.
    2. Interpretive Briefings
      Private, off-record sessions that explain the logic, rationale and lawful use of published doctrines. These briefings interpret meaning, not performance. They exist to help institutions understand doctrine, not to adapt it.
    3. Controlled Reproduction Rights
      Institutions may request permission to reproduce specific diagrams, formulas or framework excerpts within internal documentation or training materials, provided that full context and attribution are maintained.

    Access for Research and Publication

    Researchers, journalists and standards developers are encouraged to quote, link and reference Russell’s doctrines under CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 terms.

    Requests for interpretive briefings, controlled editions or reproduction rights should be made through the contact form on this site.

    Independence Statement

    All engagement follows a single rule: interpretation without alteration. No doctrine is sold, modified, or privately rewritten. Every edition remains traceable, lawful and structurally identical to its public release.
